Personal Website Portfolio and Personal Brand

For the longest time, I had nowhere to properly put my work. It lived scattered across hard drives, client folders, and half-finished Google Docs. I needed a place to archive, present, and actually own my work. So, I did what any sane person would do... I coded a whole website from scratch.

I first learned HTML and CSS at Algonquin College while studying Interactive Media Design. That foundation gave me the confidence to build my own website - and turn a scattered mess into something intentional.

Purpose of the Site:

  • Personal archive
  • Portfolio
  • Backup of all backups (not really - I have way more)
  • Make my life slightly easier

The palette is intentionally minimal. The deep blacks and muted grays create a cinematic, serious tone. The green adds personality - it’s bold, fresh, and uniquely mine. These colours carry across my website, videos, and branding to keep everything consistent.

Site Map

Wireframe Prototypes

The aesthetic follows a retro-tech vibe, inspired by my YouTube channel and personal taste. It’s clean, functional, and unmistakably mine.

What you are currently browsing on is the final version of the site - at least until I run into another problem in my life and decide to turn it into a feature. Or until I get inspired by another cool video idea. Either way, it’s a living project.
